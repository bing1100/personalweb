About Me:

No one is ever great at something right from the start. They only become great by working, learning, and improving every single day. Only over time does the perseverance of a novice become the experience of an expert. Learning is incremental and my goal is to always be learning. I want to always be moving forward; pushing myself and striving to be a better and well-rounded person. To me learning and creating is rewarding.

More than wealth and success, I want to create value for society and the community. Giving back to the community and society is a big part of who I am. I think everyone, to some degree, has confused the objectivity of wealth and success and has allowed themselves to be biased by this in their life. Although impossible to succeed in removing all bias, I don't want to forget what I believe is more important than success and wealth. I want to continue to create meaningful things and tools for myself, the people around me, and the world. Through code, hard-work and sweat, and volunteering, I believe that I will be able to change the world.

I am currently studying at the University of Waterloo in Canada. I plan to graduate with a Bachelor of Computer Science in 2018/19. I am studying computer science because I love algorithms and finding efficient and elegant solutions to problems. Creating algorithms and functions that solve complex problems is what I love to do. There is nothing better than figuring out and creating the most simple and efficient solution to a complex problem. Computer code serves as a functional solution to complex problems and is applicable in the real world. Code is powerful and is why I enjoy learning code and coding.
